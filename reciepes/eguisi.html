Eguisi

Description

Egusi soup is a rich and hearty West African dish made from ground melon seeds, which give it a thick, nutty texture. It’s typically cooked with leafy vegetables like spinach or bitterleaf, along with palm oil, spices, and a variety of meats or fish. Popular in Nigeria, it’s often served with swallow foods like pounded yam, eba, or fufu, making it a deeply satisfying and culturally significant meal.

Ingredients

  • Egusi melon
  • Palm Oil
  • Beef, dried fish, and Crayfish
  • Locust Beans
  • Spinach
  • Pepper mix
  • Onions
  • Stock Cube
  • Salt
  • Beef stock

Steps

  1. Blend the peppers and onions together - The first thing I like to do when making my Egusi soup is to blend the peppers. While blending the peppers, try to use as little water as possible so that the blended pepper will not turn out too watery. Note that, if your blended pepper turns out too watery, it will prolong the cooking time of the sauce.
  2. Blend the Egusi melon - The next thing I do is to blend the Egusi seeds, crayfish, and onions together. I also use a little bit of water for this because it’s important for the Egusi to have a thick consistency. This will allow the resulting Egusi soup to be curdled and not scattered.
  3. Heat the palm oil but don't overheat - I am not a fan of overheating palm oil. The term we usually refer to as ”bleaching” except in some recipes that the bleaching process cannot be avoided
  4. Add onions and locust bean: I added locust bean to my recipe hence, I skipped the addition of seasoning cubes. However, if you don’t like locust beans or you don’t have it handy, the stock will be sufficient to enhance the taste, or simply use the seasoning cubes to your taste. I also like to divide my locust bean into two parts. I add the first half while frying the Onions so that the flavor of the bean can get released inside the Oil. This process takes about 3 minutes.
  5. Add the blended pepper: Fry until it’s well cooked about 15 to 20 minutes, then add the Egusi melon. It is very important not to stir the Egusi at this point; give it time to curdle. Once it’s curdled for about 15 to 20 minutes, stir it briefly, then add some chicken or beef stock with the Meat or Fish of your choice and leave to cook again for another 15 minutes. The Egusi should be cooked at this point, but if you feel there is a need to cook a little longer, just add some more stock or water and cook for a few more minutes.
  6. Add the remaining locust bean, beef stock, and salt to taste. Cover and leave to cook for another 5 minutes
  7. Gently introduce the blended egusi inside the sauce but don't stir it at this point. Just cover it and leave it to cook for about 20 to 30 minutes. Keep an eye on it, so it doesn't burn but try not to stir it until the Egusi turns into a soft compact mass. Then, gently stir together and add more stock or water if necessary.
  8. Add the fish and meat and cook for another 5 minutes.
  9. Stir in the spinach and leave to simmer for about 5 minutes (stir constantly). Adjust the seasoning if necessary.
  10. Serve while still hot.
